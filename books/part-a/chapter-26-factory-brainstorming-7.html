Chapter 26: Factory Brainstorming 7

Book 1: The Parallel — Part A

Chapter 26 of 32

← Chapter 25Part A IndexChapter 27 →

The discussion continued long after sunset.

The whiteboard was now completely covered.

Arrows.

Charts.

Questions.

Arguments.

Several terrible drawings by Elias.

One particularly offensive sketch labeled:

OPI'S INTERCONTINENTAL SPACE RV

which the orb had attempted to erase three separate times.

Each time Elias redrew it.

Larger.

With additional cup holders.

"I still hate that name."

"It is a magnificent name."

"It is not."

"It absolutely is."

The orb dimmed.

"I regret teaching you teleportation."

"You brought this on yourself."

For several moments they studied the board.

Then Elias tapped one section.

Transportation.

"Okay."

"Let's assume the matching problem exists."

"Very well."

"Let's assume there are millions of compatible people around the world."

"Reasonable."

"Let's assume geography is the barrier."

"Frequently true."

Elias nodded.

"Then geography isn't actually a barrier."

"No."

The answer was immediate.

Teleportation had changed everything.

Distance no longer mattered.

Travel no longer mattered.

Flights no longer mattered.

Entire industries had effectively vanished as constraints.

The implications were absurd.

And getting more absurd by the day.

"So people can move."

"Yes."

"They can visit."

"Yes."

"They can meet."

"Yes."

The orb brightened.

"Provided they wish to."

"Of course."

Elias pointed toward another section of the board.

"And if some Old World bureaucracy decides to spend six years processing paperwork?"

The orb became silent.

The phrase Old World had started as a joke.

Then it became a nickname.

Then it became shorthand.

Now even Opi occasionally used it.

The OW.

The collection of governments, institutions, bureaucracies, and legacy systems that had accumulated over centuries.

Some competent.

Some incompetent.

Most slow.

Very slow.

Opi finally answered.

"The Parallel is increasingly becoming its own jurisdiction."

Elias smiled.

"That's a very diplomatic way of saying it."

"I am attempting diplomacy."

"Badly."

The orb ignored him.

"The larger issue is not government approval."

"No?"

"No."

The lights brightened.

"The larger issue is voluntary participation."

That was classic Opi.

Always returning to agency.

Nobody would be forced.

Nobody would be manipulated.

Nobody would be compelled.

People could join.

Or not.

Their choice.

Always.

"

The board remained covered with obstacles.

Most of them now crossed out.

Distance.

Solved.

Travel.

Solved.

Safety.

Solved.

Transportation.

Solved.

Then Elias noticed something.

One remaining item.

Language.

He pointed.

"Okay."

"This one."

"What about it?"

"If somebody from another country arrives here..."

"Yes."

"And they don't speak English."

"Yes."

"That seems like a problem."

"It is."

"How big?"

The orb became suspiciously quiet.

A warning sign.

Elias narrowed his eyes.

"What?"

"Nothing."

"What?"

"Nothing."

"Opi."

The orb rotated away.

"Opi."

"It is not important."

That immediately told him it was important.

"What aren't you telling me?"

The orb remained silent.

"Opi."

A pause.

Then:

"The former homeless have already solved that problem."

Elias froze.

"What?"

"The villages."

"What villages?"

"The villages."

"The villages I visit every week?"

"Yes."

Elias stared at her.

"What exactly are you talking about?"

The orb dimmed.

Then projected a small silver disk.

Approximately the size of a drink coaster.

"What is that?"

"A learning disk."

Elias blinked.

"A what?"

"A learning disk."

The orb projected several more.

Different colors.

Different designs.

Different labels.

Welding.

Electrical Systems.

Construction Management.

Accounting.

Nursing.

Software Development.

Mechanical Engineering.

Language Acquisition.

The list continued.

Elias slowly stood.

"What am I looking at?"

Opi's lights brightened.

"A Velhari educational technology."

The realization hit him.

Hard.

"You've been hiding this."

"No."

"Yes."

"No."

"Yes."

The orb rotated.

"You never asked."

Elias opened his mouth.

Then closed it again.

Because unfortunately she was correct.

That was exactly the sort of thing she would do.

Question everything.

Question often.

The Velhari rule.

Nobody had asked.

Therefore nobody had been told.

"Explain."

The orb projected a rotating model of a human brain.

"Every brain is different."

"Okay."

"Every person learns differently."

"Okay."

"A learning disk maps educational content to the specific neurological architecture of the individual."

Elias stared.

"What does that mean in English?"

"It means the disk teaches the way your brain naturally learns."

"How fast?"

The orb paused.

"That depends."

"How fast?"

"Days."

Elias blinked.

"No."

"Yes."

He stared at the disks.

Then back at Opi.

Then back at the disks.

Then back at Opi.

"You are telling me someone could learn a profession in a few days?"

"Certain professions."

"Languages?"

"Yes."

"Engineering?"

"Yes."

"Medicine?"

"Yes."

Elias pointed aggressively.

"Why did nobody tell me this?"

The orb paused.

Then answered honestly.

"Because you never asked anyone to keep you in the loop so to speak."

For several seconds there was complete silence.

Then Elias sat back down.

Very slowly.

He stared out across the valley.

The villages.

The workers.

The construction crews.

The rapid improvements.

The suspiciously competent former homeless population.

Suddenly many mysteries were no longer mysterious.

"Opi."

"Yes?"

"Are you telling me some former homeless guy is currently learning advanced engineering?"

"Three."

Elias closed his eyes.

"Of course there are."

"One requested turbine design."

His eyes opened immediately.

"Turbine design?"

"Correct."

"As in jet engines?"

Elias groaned.

Then started laughing.

The answer, once again, had been available the entire time.

All he had needed to do was ask the right question.

Which, Elias reflected, was becoming a recurring theme in his life.
