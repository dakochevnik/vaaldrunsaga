The following morning Elias was running again.

Which meant he was thinking again.

Which meant Opi's day was about to become worse.

Halfway through the run he stopped.

"Oh."

The answer was so obvious he almost laughed.

The husband factory.

They had been talking about it for days.

Health.

Income.

actory."

"Husband factory."

The orb dimmed.

"I already dislike where this is going."

"Attraction matters."

"Yes."

"Health matters."

"Yes."

"Money matters."

"Yes."

"Skills matter."

"Yes."

"Confidence matters."

"Yes."

Elias pointed.

"But physical appearance also matters."

The orb became quiet.

"That is difficult to dispute."

"Exactly."

He leaned forward.

"So let's say we fix everything else."

"Very well."

"We make these guys healthy."

"Yes."

"Educated."

"Yes."

"Financially secure."

"Yes."

"Emotionally stable."

"Yes."

"Purpose-driven."

"Yes."

"Great."

He paused.

"They still aren't exactly movie stars."

The orb flickered.

"Most humans are not."

"Exactly."

"So here's my question."

The orb already knew.

Which was annoying.

"Can you improve appearance?"

A pause.

Then:

"Of course."

Elias blinked.

"No hesitation?"

"None."

"Really?"

The orb rotated.

"Elias."

"Yes?"

"I can regrow limbs."

"Fair."

"I can repair spinal injuries."

"Fair."

"I can cure most diseases."

"Fair."

"I can construct personalized educational systems."

"Fair."

"I can teleport."

"Fair."

"Making someone's skin healthier is not especially challenging."

Elias nodded.

"Good point."

The orb projected a list.

Skin quality.

Dental health.

Facial symmetry.

Posture.

Body composition.

Hair restoration.

Vision correction.

Age-related deterioration.

Scarring.

Inflammation.

Sleep quality.

The list continued.

Elias stared.

"Okay."

"That's a lot."

"Yes."

"And all of this is allowed?"

That immediately changed the mood.

The Hand.

QEQO.

Question Everything.

Question Often.

The rule applied again.

Opi became thoughtful.

"Based upon historical patterns?"

"Yes."

"I believe so."

"Why?"

"Because we are not altering genetics."

The answer came instantly.

"We are not changing reproductive inheritance."

"We are not creating new traits."

"We are not redesigning humanity."

The orb brightened.

"We are improving presentation."

Elias laughed.

"Presentation?"

"Presentation."

"That's your technical term?"

"It is now."

The orb projected two images.

One showed a man who was exhausted, overweight, poorly groomed, and chronically unhealthy.

The second showed the same man healthy, rested, physically fit, and confident.

"Which image represents a violation of evolutionary processes?"

Elias looked.

"Neither."

"Correct."

The images vanished.

"However."

"There it is."

The orb ignored him.

"There are limits."

"What kind of limits?"

For the first time, Opi looked serious.

"Identity."

That wasn't the answer he expected.

"What?"

The orb floated closer.

"Humans possess a self-image."

"Okay."

"They spend decades becoming accustomed to their appearance."

"Okay."

"If changes become too extreme..."

The lights dimmed.

"...psychological adaptation becomes increasingly difficult."

Elias thought about that.

"So if somebody wakes up looking like a completely different person..."

"Problems frequently emerge."

"Interesting."

The orb nodded.

"The brain expects continuity."

"Humans are more attached to their identity than they realize."

Elias considered that.

"Okay."

"So nobody gets turned into Brad Pitt."

The orb immediately answered.

"Absolutely not."

Elias laughed.

"You've already had this conversation with someone."

"Seven times."

"Only seven?"

"This week."

He nodded.

That sounded about right.

The orb continued.

"Most individuals do not actually want perfection."

"No?"

"No."

"They want improvement."

That surprised him.

"Really?"

"Consistently."

The orb projected another graph.

"Healthy skin."

"Healthy weight."

"Good posture."

"Healthy teeth."

"Healthy hair."

"Energy."

"Confidence."

The list looked remarkably ordinary.

"No laser eyes?"

"No."

"Six-foot-eight?"

"No."

"Supermodel jawline?"

"No."

The orb brightened.

"Most people simply want to become the best version of themselves."

Elias smiled.

That actually made sense.

"So."

He leaned back.

"Husband factory plus appearance upgrades. So a beauty salon."

The orb groaned.

"I refuse to become a beauty salon."

"You're already a husband factory."

"I am not."

"You absolutely are."

"I am not."

"You are."

The orb floated away.

A clear sign the argument was over.

Which, Elias had learned, usually meant he had won.

Or at least annoyed her enough that she didn't wish to continue.

Either outcome was acceptable.

As she disappeared through the wall, her voice echoed back toward the porch.

"For the record."

"What?"

"I am not adding a spa package."

Elias grinned.

"Yet."

The wall remained silent.

Which was not technically confirmation.

But it was close enough.
