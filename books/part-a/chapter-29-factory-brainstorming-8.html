The next morning Elias went for a run.

He always thought better while moving.

The cold air helped.

The trees helped.

The lack of Opi helped.

At least temporarily.

The previous day's discussion kept replaying in his head.

Husband factories.

Matching pools.

Language disks.

The world's largest space-faring RV.

That last one still made him laugh.

Halfway through the run he slowed.

Then stopped.

"Oh."

The realization arrived suddenly.

As they often did.

He stood in the middle of the trail.

Hands on hips.

Breathing hard.

"Oh."

Two questions.

Two very important questions.

Questions they somehow hadn't asked.

Which was becoming a recurring theme.

QEQO.

Question Everything.

Question Often.

The phrase had become so common that he now shortened it automatically.

QEQO.

He smiled.

Opi would probably hate that.

Then he turned and started jogging home.

Opi was waiting on the porch.

Of course she was.

"You have questions."

"I do."

"You stopped running three point seven kilometers from the house."

"Don't make this weird."

"I've got two serious questions."

The orb brightened.

"I am listening."

"The first one is about the Hand."

Opi immediately became more attentive.

That happened every time.

Nobody took the Hand lightly.

Not even the Velhari.

Elias sat down.

"Let's assume our matching idea works."

"Proceed."

"We connect people."

"Yes."

"We expand the matching pool."

"Yes."

"We help people find spouses."

"Potentially."

"We increase births."

"Potentially."

He pointed at her.

"What does the Hand think?"

The orb was silent.

For several seconds.

Long enough that Elias knew she was taking the question seriously.

Finally she answered.

"I do not know."

"Fair."

"However."

"There it is."

The orb ignored him.

"Based upon historical patterns, I suspect the risk is low."

"Why?"

"Because we would not be forcing outcomes."

The answer came instantly.

"We would not be altering genetics."

"We would not be directing reproduction."

"We would not be modifying embryos."

"We would not be determining who reproduces."

"We would simply be increasing the probability that compatible individuals meet."

Elias considered that.

"You're saying we're not controlling evolution."

"Correct."

"We are improving discovery."

The distinction mattered.

A lot.

The orb continued.

"Historically, the Hand appears much more tolerant of increased opportunity than directed outcomes."

Elias nodded.

That sounded consistent with everything he had learned so far.

Then he smiled.

"Okay."

"That's basically the answer I expected."

The orb brightened.

"Then why ask?"

"QEQO."

The lights flickered.

"You abbreviated it."

"I did."

"I dislike that."

"I know."

Several moments passed.

Then Elias pointed at her.

"My second question is bigger."

The orb immediately became suspicious.

"How much bigger?"

"Potentially civilization-changing."

"That is not reassuring."

Elias grinned.

"Think about this."

"No."

"Come on."

"No."

"Opi."

The orb sighed.

"Proceed."

Elias leaned forward.

"Wouldn't you be the greatest matchmaker in human history?"

The orb froze.

Completely froze.

Which was unusual.

Elias immediately noticed.

"Oh."

"What?"

"You've already thought about this."

"No."

"You absolutely have."

"No."

"Yes."

The orb rotated away.

A dead giveaway.

Elias laughed.

"You're blushing."

"I am a metallic sphere."

"You're emotionally blushing."

"I am not."

"You are."

The orb ignored him.

Because unfortunately he was correct.

She had already considered it.

Many times.

Long before Elias had.

"Think about it," Elias continued.

"You know everybody."

"No."

"You know practically everybody."

"Also no."

"You know more about people than any human who has ever lived."

"Closer."

He pointed triumphantly.

"Exactly."

The orb became quiet.

Elias continued.

"You know personalities."

"Yes."

"Values."

"Yes."

"Goals."

"Yes."

"Psychology."

"Yes."

"Compatibility."

"Partially."

"Communication styles."

"Yes."

"Conflict tendencies."

"Often."

He leaned back.

"So why are we acting like this is difficult?"

The orb dimmed.

"Because humans are not equations."

"Fair."

"Because attraction is not entirely predictable."

"Fair."

"Because free will exists."

"Fair."

"Because individuals routinely make decisions contrary to their own interests."

"Very fair."

The orb brightened again.

"However."

"There it is again."

"However."

The word lingered.

Elias smiled.

"Go on."

The orb projected a web of connections into the air.

Millions of them.

Potential friendships.

Potential business partnerships.

Potential marriages.

Potential families.

Potential futures.

"I could improve the odds."

Elias immediately pointed.

"Exactly."

"I could identify likely compatibility."

"Exactly."

"I could identify likely conflicts."

"Exactly."

"I could identify individuals likely to share values."

"Exactly."

"I could identify individuals likely to create stable families."

"Exactly."

The orb paused.

Then added:

"Though I would never choose for them."

The statement arrived instantly.

Firm.

Certain.

Velhari.

Agency mattered.

Always.

Elias nodded.

"Of course."

"You may introduce people."

"Yes."

"You may improve the odds."

"Yes."

"You may remove barriers."

"Yes."

"But they still choose."

"Correct."

The web continued glowing between them.

Millions of possibilities.

Millions of futures.

Then Elias asked the obvious question.

"Would the Hand care about that?"

The orb became quiet again.

This time for even longer.

Finally she answered.

"Again, I suspect not."

"Why?"

"Because matching is not breeding."

The answer surprised him.

The orb continued.

"A matchmaker creates opportunities."

"A breeder creates outcomes."

The distinction landed immediately.

One respected agency.

The other replaced it.

One expanded choice.

The other constrained it.

The Hand appeared to care very deeply about that difference.

For several moments neither spoke.

Then Elias smiled.

"So basically you're telling me you're Bumble."

"No."

"Yes."

"No."

"Bumble with godlike powers."

"No."

"Bumble with alien powers."

"No."

The orb brightened.

"I am not becoming a dating website."

Elias grinned.

"That's exactly what a dating website would say."

For some reason Opi immediately left the porch.

Which, Elias noticed, was becoming her standard response whenever he was right.

Or when she suspected he might be.

Both occurred far too often for her liking.
